Planning Your Journey

  • Plan your route and identify transfer stations ahead of time.
  • Load your Beep Card to avoid long ticket booth queues.
  • Check the train schedule before leaving to minimize waiting times.

Safety Tips

  • Always stay behind the yellow line on the platform until the train comes to a complete stop.
  • Hold onto railings when standing to avoid falls during sudden stops.
  • Be aware of your surroundings to protect your belongings from theft.

During Rush Hours

Try to travel during non-peak hours (usually mid-morning or early afternoon) to avoid crowds and long lines.

Follow these tips for a smoother and safer train ride.

1. Use Beep Cards for Faster Entry

Avoid long lines by using a Beep Card instead of single-journey tickets. Load your card in advance to prevent delays.

2. Travel During Off-Peak Hours

Trains are less crowded before 7 AM and after 9 PM. Avoid peak hours (7-9 AM, 5-8 PM) for a more comfortable ride.

3. Stand on the Right, Walk on the Left

When using escalators, stand on the right to let others pass on the left.

4. Follow the 1-Meter Rule

Stay at least 1 meter away from the train doors while waiting to board.

5. Watch Out for Pickpockets

Keep your bag zipped and close to you, especially in crowded areas. Be cautious when using your phone.

6. Line Up Properly When Boarding

Let passengers exit first before boarding to avoid congestion.

7. Take the First or Last Car if You Have Luggage

Train cars at the front and back are usually less crowded, making them ideal for passengers with large bags.

8. Use Station Facilities

Some stations offer free water refilling stations and clean restrooms for your convenience.

9. Plan for Train Delays

Unexpected delays happen. If you have an important appointment, leave earlier than planned.
